# Go-To-Market Strategy
## Namastey Udaipur — Pilot Launch
*Author: Nikhil Tiwari | March 2015*

---

## GTM Philosophy

We are not building a mass-market travel app in year one. We are building a trusted tool for a specific, underserved community: domestic Indian travelers who don't speak Hindi or English as their primary language.

Our GTM strategy therefore is **community-first, not channel-first.** We go to where these communities already exist and earn trust there, before spending on acquisition.

---

## Target Community Segments (MVP)

| Segment | Size (Udaipur annually) | Acquisition Channel |
|---|---|---|
| Tamil Nadu travelers | ~15,000 est. | Tamil travel groups, AIESEC Tamil volunteers |
| Bengali travelers | ~12,000 est. | Bengali cultural associations, AIESEC Bengal |
| South Indian travelers (broad) | ~40,000 est. | South Indian travel Facebook groups |
| AIESEC member network | ~2,000 members across India | Direct AIESEC network |

---

## Phase 1 GTM: Community Seeding (March – June 2015)

### Channel 1: Cold Email to Regional Tourist Groups

**Execution:** Identify organized regional tourist groups, travel clubs, and cultural associations. Send personalized cold emails explaining the product and offering early access.

**Example:** Tamil Nadu Tourism Club (Facebook group, 3,400 members) → contacted admin → offered free guide booking for first 10 Tamil travelers who install the app.

**Results:** Primary acquisition channel. ~40% email open rate. Drove bulk of early installs.

**Template approach:**
> "We built a travel app specifically for Tamil travelers visiting Udaipur. Your restaurant reviews help the next Tamil family who visits. First 10 Tamil travelers get a free guide booking through our AIESEC network."

---

### Channel 2: AIESEC India Network

**Execution:** Co-founder's AIESEC connections used to spread app awareness through AIESEC India's network of 5,000+ members across 22 cities. AIESEC members = well-traveled, multilingual, exactly our target profile.

**Results:** Direct installs + credibility signal. AIESEC members became early reviewers and content contributors.

---

### Channel 3: Local Hotel Partnerships

**Execution:** Approached 5 Udaipur hotels with pitch: "Tell your guests about this free app. It helps Tamil and Bengali guests feel at home. It'll improve their stay rating."

**Results:** 2 hotels agreed to recommend the app at check-in. Low volume but high-intent users.

---

## Phase 2 GTM: Tourist Arrival Moments (June – October 2015)

**Insight:** The highest-intent moment to download a city travel app is *at the destination* — train station, bus stand, hotel lobby.

**Execution:** Printed QR code cards distributed at:
- Udaipur railway station (partnered with a chai stall owner)
- City Palace ticket counter area
- 2 partner hotels' reception desks

**Targeting:** Cards printed in Tamil, Bengali, and Hindi with tagline in each language.

**Results:** Limited traction. QR code scanning behavior low in 2015 India. Revised to WhatsApp share link in October 2015.

---

## Phase 3 GTM: Community Reviews Flywheel (October 2015 – January 2016)

**Strategy:** Each review left by a Tamil traveler is shared back to Tamil travel Facebook groups with: "Karthik from Chennai reviewed Annapurna in Udaipur on Namastey Udaipur. 12 Tamil travelers have now reviewed places there."

**Execution:** Manual social sharing of aggregate community reviews. Designed to create FOMO and social proof within regional travel communities.

**Results:** Early positive signal but manual effort wasn't sustainable. Needed automated social sharing feature.

## Monetization GTM

### Guide Booking Commission (Active in MVP)
- 15-20% commission on completed guide bookings
- Guide acquisition: AIESEC network + direct outreach to Udaipur guides
- 8 confirmed bookings in pilot = proof of monetization

### Hotel Partnership (Planned — v2)
- Approach hotels with South Indian/Bengali amenities for featured placement
- Offer "certified culturally-friendly" badge in exchange for confirmed amenities
- Commission on hotel bookings through app

### SaaS for Hotels (Planned — v3)
- Hotels in Rajasthan buy access to "cultural guest communication" tool
- Pre-arrival WhatsApp in guest's language, food preference flagging, check-in preferences
- Target: Hotels with 20%+ South Indian guest mix

---

## Metrics That Defined GTM Success

1. **Install-to-language-selection rate** — are the right people finding us?
2. **Regional review submission rate** — are users contributing to the community?
3. **Guide booking conversion** — does the product earn?
4. **Cross-city demand signal** — are users asking "when for Jaipur?" (expansion signal)

---

*GTM strategy executed by co-founders without marketing budget. All acquisition organic or community-driven.*
