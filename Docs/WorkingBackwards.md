## PRESS RELEASE

**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

### Namastey Udaipur Launches India's First Culturally-Personalized Travel App, Letting Every Indian Traveler Experience Any City in Their Own Language and Culture

*New platform uses community-powered regional recommendations to make domestic travel feel like home, regardless of where you're from*

**UDAIPUR, RAJASTHAN** — Namastey Udaipur today launched a travel discovery and booking platform that for the first time allows Indian tourists to explore Udaipur — and eventually any Indian city — through the lens of their own language, cuisine preferences, and cultural identity.

Unlike existing travel platforms that present the same English or Hindi content to every user, Namastey Udaipur surfaces restaurants reviewed by travelers from your own state, hotels that serve the food you grew up with, and local guides who speak your language — all within an app that operates entirely in your mother tongue.

"When a Tamil family visits Udaipur, they don't want to feel like foreigners in their own country," said [Co-Founder], who built the platform after watching 60 elderly Hindi-speaking Indians struggle to enjoy a Europe tour because every sign, every audio guide, and every menu was in a language they didn't understand. "We're solving that problem for India — so that a grandmother from Patna can travel to Udaipur and feel as comfortable as she does at home."

The platform launches with support for Tamil, Bengali, Hindi, and English, with plans to expand to all 22 scheduled Indian languages. Content is curated and authenticated by a partnership with AIESEC India, a global youth volunteer network with members from every Indian state and dozens of international backgrounds.

Key features include culturally-filtered restaurant discovery, regional community reviews, hotel matching by cultural amenities (such as South Indian breakfast or halal options), local guide booking, and offline access for low-connectivity areas of the city.

Namastey Udaipur is available now on Android and Web at namasteyudaipur.com.

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

### Customer Questions

**Q: How is this different from TripAdvisor or MakeMyTrip?**

TripAdvisor and MakeMyTrip are excellent platforms for booking and reading general reviews. But their content is overwhelmingly in English, written by English-comfortable travelers, and optimized for a culturally generic audience. If you're a Tamil traveler and you read that a restaurant is "great for North Indian food lovers" — that review tells you nothing. Namastey Udaipur shows you what Tamil travelers specifically said about that restaurant, whether they found it suitable for their palate, and whether it serves filter coffee. That's a fundamentally different signal.

**Q: What if there aren't many reviews from my region yet?**

We've partnered with AIESEC India to seed authentic regional content before launch. AIESEC volunteers from every Indian state have personally visited, evaluated, and reviewed content in Udaipur. You'll see community-validated content from day one, not an empty app. As more travelers from your region use the platform, the content gets richer and more personalized.

**Q: Can I use this app if I don't have internet access in parts of the city?**

Udaipur's old city and some heritage areas have inconsistent cellular coverage. Namastey Udaipur caches core content — your saved restaurants, your guide's contact, your hotel confirmation — so the information you need is available even when you lose signal. We're actively improving offline functionality based on user feedback.

**Q: How do I book a local guide?**

Tap "Book a Guide" in the app. You'll see profiles of verified local guides with their languages spoken, tour specialties, pricing, and community ratings. Select a guide and submit a booking request — we'll confirm via callback within 2 hours. All guides in the MVP are verified through the AIESEC India network.

**Q: What languages does the app support?**

Tamil, Bengali, Hindi, and English at launch. We're actively expanding to Punjabi, Marathi, Kannada, Telugu, Malayalam, and Gujarati. If your language isn't supported yet, the English version is fully functional while we build out your language pack.

### Internal / Business Questions

**Q: Why Udaipur first? Why not Delhi or Mumbai?**

Three reasons. First, Udaipur has very high domestic tourist volume with visitors from across India — it's a good proxy for pan-India linguistic diversity. Second, the manageable geography allows us to manually curate high-quality content before building automated systems. Third, this is where we are — local knowledge gives us a significant advantage in content accuracy and guide network quality. We prove the model here, then scale.

**Q: What's the monetization model?**

Three revenue streams, prioritized for phased rollout:
1. **Commission on guide bookings** — 15–20% commission on each guide booking completed through the platform (active in MVP)
2. **Hotel and restaurant booking commission** — 8–12% on bookings made through integrated booking flow (v2)
3. **Cultural concierge SaaS for hotels** — white-label version for hotels to offer culturally-personalized pre-arrival communication to their guests (v3)

**Q: What's the biggest risk to this business?**

Content quality and cold start. The entire product thesis depends on authentic, regionally-specific reviews. If we have fake reviews, thin content, or culturally inaccurate recommendations, users will lose trust and never return. The AIESEC partnership is our solution to cold start, but maintaining content quality as we scale is the hardest unsolved problem.

**Q: How do you scale content to 22 languages across multiple cities?**

Phase 1: AIESEC volunteer network for seed content + manual curation
Phase 2: Community translation tools allowing bilingual users to contribute translations
Phase 3: LLM-assisted content translation with community validation layer
Phase 4: Full UGC platform with regional community moderators

**Q: Who is the long-term competitor you're most worried about?**

Google. If Google Maps adds regional language filtering and cultural review segmentation, they have the distribution, the data, and the brand to execute this faster than us. Our moat is the community — the Tamil travelers who trust Namastey Udaipur because it was built for them, not bolted onto a generic platform as a feature. Community loyalty is harder to replicate than features.
